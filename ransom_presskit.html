Synopsis

Ran Som is a comedy thriller that turns a crime gone wrong into an unexpected story of connection. Two small-time burglars kidnap the son of a wealthy businessman, hoping for a quick payout. What begins as a clumsy, ill-planned heist shifts when they uncover the boy's troubled home life. As tensions rise, moments of humor and unease collide, revealing that everyone — whether criminal or respectable — is shaped by circumstance. The final encounter leaves no clear winners or villains, only a fleeting sense that even in chaos, people can choose to see each other differently.

Writer & Director
Richard Dang
Writer & Director

Richard Dang is a Cambodian Canadian filmmaker whose work explores inner guilt, peace amid violence, and intergenerational reconciliation. Since 2021, he's worked as a union Assistant Director, learning the craft from the ground up. When the 2023 WGA and SAG-AFTRA strikes occurred, his love for storytelling pushed him into the director's chair.


Since then, Richard has written and directed twenty films to create space for himself and his peers to express themselves. His work has screened at many Canadian festivals. By connecting with festivals, he hopes to bring these stories to wider audiences and continue expanding the reach of his films.

Director's Notes

Ran Som grew out of a real experience from my childhood, when two men broke into my home while I was alone. They were from my own community — familiar faces, not movie villains. Years later, I began to understand what desperation and environment can do to people.

This film blends tension with dark humour to explore that gray area — how empathy sometimes appears in the most unlikely situations. The burglars in Ran Som aren't evil, just lost. The father isn't simply cruel, just repeating what he knows. By the end, each character sees the others differently, and for a brief moment, there's room for change. That space between laughter and unease — that's where the humanity of this story lives.

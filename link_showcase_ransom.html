RAN SOM

Two small-time burglars kidnap the son of a wealthy businessman, demanding twenty thousand dollars. But when they discover that the boy is a victim of parental abuse, they face a moral dilemma that blurs the line between criminals and protectors.

drama, comedy 12 mins.

Director Statement

When I was seven years old, I experienced a home invasion while home alone. The men who broke in were from my community, people I recognized. Years later, I made mistakes of my own, and began to see how cycles of criminality repeat.

I grew up in a tight-knit Cambodian community and realized, later, how little guidance we had. Our parents were often uneducated country folks, affected by war and genocide, trying to figure things out. At home, discipline was harsh, an attempt to "teach" us. Meanwhile, my peers and I were out in the world, trying to see what works and what doesn't, like blind mice, bumping and scratching into one another, trying to get figure things out.

This film is about mistakes and agency. It is about choosing peace over violence, and taking responsibility not only for ourselves but also for the next generation. This is a much needed love letter to my community, to forgive our mistakes so we can do better.
